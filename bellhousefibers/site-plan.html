Techniques to Know

Do not let stitches intimidate you. The best way to learn new stitches is by doing a swatch. What is a swatch? A swatch is your friend. Look at any pattern and you will see an option to create a swatch. An example: Using size 5 needles, knit 20 stitches across and 10 rows down. This should measure as a 3x3 swatch.This can help you understand not only your tension but also how the colors blend together.

Practice Makes Beautiful

It just takes a little practice to learn how to knit or crochet. Making time to learn is essential. Even if it's only 20 minutes a day, you can do it. Start with something easy, like stockenette stitch or a single crochet pattern. Give knitting a try. If you don't feel comfortable with 2 needles move to 1 needle and learn the basics of crochet. Before you know it you'll be confident creating more advanced stitches like a left or right cable or bobble stitch. Click here to download a free instruction infographic to knit your first swatch.

Here is a list of essential items to have on hand so you can learn to knit with confidence. -A comfy chair -Good lighting -Reading glasses or a magnifier -Written pattern instructions (If you want to learn how to read a picture pattern click here) -Neddles- Double Pointed or Circular (A note about neddles: use one size larger than what the pattern states if you are a tight knitter.) -Practice Yarn- (This yarn does not have to be the expensive stuff. The History of Knitting and Crocheting

Irish immigrants funded their journey to America because of their skills in knitting and crocheting. Knitting became more and more popular because of Queen Victoria. She learned to knit and spin yarn and shared her creations with family and friends. Woman in the early 1900s considered the intricate patterns of lace work to be the height of fashion. These skills passed down to future generations should be treasured. Take a trip down memory lane and see if your ancestors were knitters or crocheters. Look in the attic, you have some knitted or crocheted family heirlooms.

My Favorite Pattern

Do you have a favorite pattern? There is one pattern that I will always try no matter what yarn I'm using. It's easy, relaxing and you'll have a shawl knitted within a weekend. Here is the simple 2 row repeat. CO5 - Cast On 5 Stitches. (kfb = knit front back) (k2tog = knit 2 stitches together) R1 - Row #1 k1, kfb, knit to end of row. R2 - Row #2 k1, k2tog, knit to second to last stitch, kfb, k1. That's it, easy peasy! You can use a stitch marker to help you know which side you are on.
